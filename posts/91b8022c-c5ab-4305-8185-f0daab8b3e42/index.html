How to become an expert

  • Dec 26, 2023
  • Jan 10, 2026
  • 1 min

You get pretty good at something by a mixture of study and practice. You get to be an expert by building up an extensive library of explicit and tacit knowledge. Traditionally an expert is made through study (for the explicit) and experience (for the tacit). They do the relevant thing for a long time and thereby gaining a library of situations and experience which the expert draws on, consciously and unconsciously, to make decisions.

What’s the most effective way to acquire explicit and tacit knowledge? The answer depends on the subject matter. Use Deliberate practice for subjects with a “long history of well-established pedagogy” (Chin 2020) (for example: math, music, and chess). Use Purposeful practice for other subjects (for example: business, sales, sport, art, singing, writing, poetry, etc).

Expertise, and tacit knowledge by extension, boils down to pattern matching, specifically Recognition-primed decision making via a mental library of Frames. Improve by working through simulations (Prefer simulations that already exist) or real-world experience (e.g. apprenticeship / on-the-job experience). Use Applied cognitive task analysis to extract expertise from specific experts.
