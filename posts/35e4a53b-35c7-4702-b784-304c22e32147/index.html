Work smart and hard

  • Nov 22, 2023
  • Jan 10, 2026
  • 2 min

‘Work smart, not hard’ is Lowercase-a accurate without being capital-T True. Yes, one could achieve the same with less effort by being smart about when, where, how, etc, they applied that effort. But that’s not how it’s often done in practice. I’ve more often seen, with others and myself, this phrase used as if it meant “Working smart means I don’t have to work hard”. That’s the inaccuracy. You’ll still have to work hard. Maybe not as hard, or maybe for not as long, but still: you’ll work hard.

I recommend working both smart and hard. Be intelligent about how you apply your effort but remember that you still need to put in effort.

[…] if you want to excel, then you have to work hard and focus the whole time.

Smart guy productivity pitfalls

fucking around for three days and then making a herculean effort on day four that restores your average progress to what a moderately inept person would have achieved with consistent effort, that’s what it’s all about.

(argumate n.d.)

I believe that being a hardo, or a try-hard, or a sweat, is good, actually.

The differentiating factor in people’s success that I see most is the amount of effort that an individual is willing to put into whatever endeavor they are pursuing.

(Bateman 2024)
